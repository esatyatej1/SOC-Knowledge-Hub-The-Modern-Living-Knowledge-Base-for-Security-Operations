# Incident Response Lifecycle

## The NIST Incident Response Steps
Proper IR follow a structured process to ensure no step is missed during the heat of an investigation.

1. **Preparation:** Establishing tools, training, and processes before an incident happens.
2. **Detection & Analysis:** Identifying signs of a breach and determining its scope.
3. **Containment, Eradication, & Recovery:**
   - **Containment:** Stopping the spread (e.g., isolating a host).
   - **Eradication:** Removing the threat (e.g., deleting malware).
   - **Recovery:** Restoring systems to normal (e.g., restoring from backups).
4. **Post-Incident Activity:** Lessons learned and root cause analysis (RCA).

## Common Incident Types in SOC
- **Phishing:** Email-based credential harvesting or malware delivery.
- **Ransomware:** Data encryption and extortion.
- **Brute Force / Password Spraying:** Identity-based attacks.
- **Data Exfiltration:** Unauthorized transfer of sensitive data.
- **Vulnerability Exploitation:** Attacking unpatched systems.
