# Threat Intelligence Basics

## What is Threat Intelligence?
Threat Intelligence (TI) is evidence-based knowledge, including context, mechanisms, indicators, implications, and actionable advice, about an existing or emerging menace or hazard to assets.

## The TI Lifecycle
1. **Direction:** Defining requirements based on business risk.
2. **Collection:** Gathering data from open sources (OSINT), closed sources, and technical feeds.
3. **Processing:** Normalizing and de-duplicating data.
4. **Analysis & Production:** Turning raw data into actionable intelligence.
5. **Dissemination:** Getting the intelligence to the right people (SOC, IR, Leadership).
6. **Feedback:** Refining the process based on usage.

## Types of Intelligence
- **Strategic:** High-level trends for decision-makers (CISO).
- **Operational:** Intelligence about specific attacks and campaigns (SOC Manager).
- **Tactical:** Real-time data about tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) (Analyst).
- **Technical:** Specific Indicators of Compromise (IoCs) (SIEM/XDR).

## Common Formats
- **STIX (Structured Threat Information Expression):** Standardized language for sharing TI.
- **TAXII (Trusted Automated Exchange of Intelligence Information):** Protocol for exchanging TI over HTTPS.
